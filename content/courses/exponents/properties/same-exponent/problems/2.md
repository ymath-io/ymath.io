@statement
Expand and simplify $a(bcd)^e$.

@hint
Group variables and apply the Power of a Product Rule twice.

@solution
We first apply the Power of a Product Rule on $b$ and $cd$, then on $c$ and $d$.
$$a(bcd)^e
= a(b(cd))^e
= a(b^e(cd)^e)
=a(b^e(c^ed^e))
=ab^ec^ed^e$$
